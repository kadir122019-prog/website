01 / Operating System

AstineOS
Built from the ground up.

In active development · 3 years

A Unix-like operating system for x86_64 — and soon aarch64 — built entirely from scratch. Custom kernel, custom desktop environment, custom package manager. No compromises, no corporate agenda.

Core Components
01

pkine — Package Manager

A fully self-written package manager for AstineOS. pkine handles installation, removal, and management of programs and system services. Currently in active development — the goal is a fast, simple, dependency-aware package system that doesn't get in your way.

02

Custom Desktop Environment

No third-party DE. AstineOS ships with a desktop environment designed and built as part of the OS itself — ensuring deep integration, consistent behavior, and a cohesive user experience that can evolve alongside the system.

03

Unix-like Foundation

AstineOS follows Unix principles — everything is a file, small composable tools, clear separation of concerns. Familiar to anyone who has used Linux or BSD, but built from scratch with modern goals in mind.

04

aarch64 Roadmap

The OS is being developed with future ARM support in mind. This pairs directly with the Astine ØNE laptop — eventually, AstineOS will be the native OS for Astine hardware on ARM silicon.

Design Principles
01

Hot-Swappable Battery

The defining feature. A 2S Li-Ion battery pack that can be removed and replaced without powering down. Running low on a long day? Carry a spare pack and keep going. No proprietary charger required. No "battery health" anxiety after 500 cycles.

02

Full Aluminium Chassis

The body is machined aluminium — not a thin plastic shell that cracks or flexes. Individual panels are designed to be replaceable, so a dent or scratch doesn't mean a new laptop. The structure is meant to last years without degrading.

03

ARM Efficiency

ARM architecture was chosen deliberately for its power efficiency. The goal is real-world battery life that holds up — not a lab benchmark number. Combined with the swappable battery system, the ØNE is built to get through any day.

04

Engineered Cooling

Thermal design is taken seriously. The ØNE's cooling system is engineered around real sustained workloads — not just to pass a short thermal test. The chassis material and internal layout were chosen with heat dissipation as a primary constraint.

03 / About

Why
this exists.

"I got tired of being told to accept a laptop that's disposable by design."

I'm a 15-year-old high school student from Austria. For the past three years, I've been designing an operating system and a laptop — not as a school project, not for a competition — because I genuinely believe the products people rely on every day could be so much better.

It started with frustration. Every laptop I looked at was a sealed, non-repairable unit: buy it, use it for a few years, throw it away when the battery health drops or the chassis gets dented. Framework tried to fix this — I respect what they're doing — but I wasn't satisfied. I had different ideas about what repairability and longevity could look like.

So I started working on it myself. AstineOS began as an attempt to understand operating systems from the inside out — kernel, package management, desktop environment, all of it written from scratch. The Astine ØNE came out of asking: what would a laptop look like if it was designed around the person using it, not around cost optimization?

This isn't a business in the traditional sense. I'm not trying to capture market share or hit revenue targets. I want to make something people genuinely love — something that listens to what users actually say about their machines and tries to address it. If someone says "my battery dies too fast," the answer shouldn't be a slightly bigger battery. It should be a battery you can swap out in seconds.

I'm building in public, learning as I go, and trying to make both AstineOS and the ØNE actually functional and affordable. This is a long road — but I've already been on it for three years, and I'm not stopping.

— Astine

04 / Roadmap

Goals
What comes next.

Both AstineOS and the Astine ØNE are in active development. Here's what the roadmap looks like — in order of what needs to happen first.

01

Finish pkine Package Manager

Complete the core of pkine — dependency resolution, package installation, service management, and a usable repository format. This is the foundation everything else depends on.

In Progress
02

Make AstineOS Daily-Driveable

Reach a point where AstineOS can be used as a primary OS for real, everyday tasks — stable, with working drivers, a functional DE, and enough software available through pkine to cover daily workflows.

Planned
03

aarch64 Architecture Support

Port AstineOS to ARM (aarch64) — a necessary step for running natively on the Astine ØNE hardware and expanding the OS to the ARM ecosystem.

Planned
04

Astine ØNE — First Functional Prototype

Build a working prototype of the ØNE laptop — functional hot-swap battery, aluminium chassis, ARM SoC, and cooling system. Not a final product, but something real you can hold and use.

Planned
05

Make People Satisfied

Listen to feedback, fix what's broken, and keep improving. The goal isn't to ship something and move on — it's to build something people actually enjoy using and come back to. Satisfaction isn't a milestone, it's an ongoing commitment.

Ongoing
06

Build a Community

Grow a community of people who care about open, repairable, user-first computing. The Discord is the starting point — the goal is a space where users can give real feedback, contributors can help build, and everyone owns a piece of what Astine becomes.

Growing
07

Reasonable Pricing

Make the Astine ØNE available at a price that reflects its quality without being out of reach. This isn't about maximizing margin — it's about making this accessible to the people who need it.

Long-term
